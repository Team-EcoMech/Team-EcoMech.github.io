Introduction

With global energy demands rising, the search for renewable energy sources has become critical. Innovative solutions are needed to harness energy efficiently and sustainably.
This project aims to explore the feasibility and efficiency of integrating Vertical Axis Wind Turbines (VAWTs) along expressways in Sri Lanka to harness wind energy generated by passing vehicles and natural wind flows. Recognizing the untapped potential of wind energy in transportation corridors, our initiative seeks to design, simulate, and analyze small-scale VAWTs optimized explicitly for installation in the median strips of highways.

Project Features

Energy Capture Technology

Utilizes innovative wind turbines designed for optimal energy capture from highway winds.
Systems engineered to maximize the conversion of vehicular-induced airflow into renewable energy.

Simulation and Analysis

Employs advanced computational fluid dynamics (CFD) simulations to predict and enhance turbine performance.
Data-driven design process ensures efficient and practical turbine implementation.

Sustainable Impact

Reduces reliance on fossil fuels by integrating renewable energy generation into highway infrastructure.
Contributes to environmental sustainability and energy decentralization.

Interactive Monitoring System

Features a user-friendly interface for real-time monitoring and management of turbine systems.
Provides instant access to performance metrics and operational status.

Project Impacts

Innovative Energy Solutions

Introduces cutting-edge wind turbine technology for highway environments.
Pioneers the use of highways as conduits for sustainable energy production.

Economic Viability

Focuses on cost-effective solutions with potential for significant return on investment through energy production.
Enhances the economic feasibility of using renewable energy technologies in non-traditional settings.

Environmental Benefits

Contributes to reduced carbon emissions and promotes a cleaner environment.
Helps mitigate the environmental impact of traditional energy sources.

Educational and Research Advancements

Provides a platform for ongoing research and development in renewable energy technologies.
Offers educational opportunities for engineering and environmental studies.

Methodology

Case Study Details
  • Case study on a segment of the Outer Circular Highway, focusing on traffic, wind, and space.
  • Creating small-scale VAWTs using CAD, tailored for highway-generated wind patterns.
  • Analyzing VAWT performance and energy efficiency with CFD under varied wind conditions.
  • Examining impacts on wildlife, vehicle safety, and noise levels.
  • Evaluating the economic feasibility, maintenance requirements, and expected energy output.
